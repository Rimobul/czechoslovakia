# Introduction

Since the split of Czechoslovakia in 1993, both countries have taken their own economic paths. The Czech Republic maintained its position as the stronger economy, while Slovakia experienced dynamic growth especially after joining the European Union.

## Historical Context

At the time of the split, Slovakia was industrially less developed than the Czech lands. Heavy industry and arms manufacturing, on which Slovakia depended, were undergoing painful restructuring.

## Key Findings

1. **Czech Republic** maintained higher absolute GDP
2. **Slovakia** showed faster growth rates in 2000-2010
3. Both countries converged after EU accession

## Current State

Today, both economies are closely interconnected. Mutual trade represents a significant part of both countries' GDP, and many companies operate in both markets.

### Interesting Numbers

- GDP per capita CZ: ~25,000 EUR
- GDP per capita SK: ~21,000 EUR
- The gap is gradually narrowing

## Conclusion

The economic stories of both nations show that different paths can lead to similar goals. Slovakia managed to significantly catch up with its neighbor thanks to reforms and favorable investment policies.
